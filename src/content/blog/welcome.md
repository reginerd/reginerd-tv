Welcome,

If you're reading this and your name isn't Reggie Cooper, why?

This simple astro blog is going to serve as an outlet for me to express my thoughts on music I've been enjoying or consuming. Over the years I've felt that I've been somewhat losing my connection with the media I consume, the technology and tools I work with, and actually critically thinking about these hobbies I was once enamored by. 

I grew up really enjoying and collecting music at a young age. The first album I remember purcashing was Nelly's Country Grammar (Clean Version) with my dad. I vividly remember spinning through his CD tower and looking at the cover art and track listings. Birthday and holiday wishlists often consisted of albums from artists I enjoyed. And once the MP3 players launched, it was a wrap. Shit... and don't get me started on Limewire and Bearshare. I was always making mixes and in middle school I was one of the kids helping others get music on their players. (I honestly didn't even remember that until I started typing this. damn lol)

Anyway, as time and technology has evolved, I've slowly stopped purchasing CDs. I think the last <i>big</i> album release day I remember was June 18, 2013 when Yeezus, Born Sinner, and Watching Movies With The Sound Off were released. I went to target at 8AM to grab all three. I requested the day off from work just to listen to the albums and really absorb them. 

Over time I've continued collecting music, expanding into records and video game soundtracks. (Frostpunk is GOATED - we'll talk about that later) While I've continued purchasing and collecting music, my main consumption method has been streaming services. I've tried 'em all - Apple Music, Spotify, even TIDAL. I really gave TIDAL a shot, man. I don't know how many months, but a lot of 'em, on the highest tier. I've always respected and liked the idea of supporting the artists I listen to most. It's actually kind of the reason why we're here... 

My most recent Spotify Wrapped results show that I'm listening to the music that I already own. Clipse, Tyler, Kendrick, Key Glock... These three artists are my "on sight" releases. I don't need to see or hear what they have coming out, I am going to purchase it. (Maybe even multiple times lol) They occupy my wrapped year over year. Why haven't I just been listening to own shit? Why was I paying these corpos this money to rent music. Trippin...

January 2026, I finally cut the cord with my music streaming service. I'm back to collecting CDs and purchasing digital downloads. I am ripping and archiving my collection to my NAS, where I consume it through Plex currently. (I was trying out a DAP, but it didn't have gapless playback) While I build up my collection, I want to be more intentional when I listen to it. I plan to use this page and instagram to express my thoughts about the album. Shit's probably gonna be a little scuffed at least to begin with, but I plan to build it out over time.

To try to kill many birds with one stone, I am using this as an opportunity to do things like sharpen my creative (written, artistic, maybe spoken?) and technology skills. Let's see how it goes.

-Reggie
